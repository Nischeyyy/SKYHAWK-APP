You can absolutely achieve this style. The Apple Sports app is one of the best examples of Apple's 2026 design language.

However, **don't copy it 1:1**. Apple can enforce its own design language; third-party apps that mimic it too closely risk looking derivative. Instead, borrow the principles and adapt them to Skyhawk.

## I would go one step further

I would make the app feel like **Apple built a security operations app**.

That means:

* Apple Sports-style navigation and cards.
* Wallet-style credential presentation.
* Fitness-style activity rings for hours worked, certifications, or weekly shifts.
* Maps-style bottom sheets for shift details and incident reporting.
* Journal-style generous spacing and typography.

Done well, this would feel like a first-party iOS app while still having a distinct Skyhawk identity, rather than looking like a direct clone of Apple Sports.
